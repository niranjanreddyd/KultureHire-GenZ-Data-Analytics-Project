Generation Z (Gen Z) refers to people born roughly between 1997 and 2012, following Millennials. Gen Z is known for being the first generation to grow up with widespread access to the internet, social media, and mobile technology from a young age. Here are some key characteristics associated with Gen Z:
Digital Natives
Social Awareness

Data Integrity and Security:

Critical for ensuring accurate data transmission and preventing corruption.

Relevant to both Gen Z and employers in a digital and data-driven world.

Gen Z's Awareness:

Proficient in technology but may lack understanding of technical aspects like error detection.

Education in networking, data communication, and cybersecurity is essential.

Impact on Daily Life:

Data integrity issues affect everyday digital experiences, such as file corruption and communication errors.

Employer's Perspective:

Data-driven decision-making relies on accurate and error-free data.
